 LUXEMBOURG
 Luxembourg is a parliamentary democracy under a con-
 stitutional monarchy, in which human rights are
 scrupulously respected. Luxembourg is a party to
 the 1950 European Convention for the Protection of Human
 Rights and Fundamental Freedoms.
 1. Respect for the Integrity of the Person, Including
 Freedom from:
 a. Torture
 Freedom from torture is guaranteed by law and
 respected in practice.
 b. Cruel, Inhuman or Degrading Treatment or
 Punishment
 Freedom from cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment or
 punishment is guaranteed by law and respected in prac-
 tice. Luxembourg is building a new, modern prison
 facility that should be completed in 1980. Prisoners
 are allowed visitors and choice of attorneys.
 c. Arbitrary Arrest or Imprisonment
 Freedom from arbitrary arrest or imprisonment and the
 right of habeas corpus are guaranteed by law and
 respected in practice. Detention is permitted without
 a hearing for a period of no longer than 24 hours.
 There are no political prisoners.
 d. Denial of Fair Public Trial
 Fair public trial is guaranteed by independent, civil
 courts. There is no death penalty.
 e. Invasion of the Home
 Freedom from invasion of the home is guaranteed by
 law and respected in practice.
 2. Governmental Policies Relating to the Fulfillment
 of Such Vital Needs as Food, Shelter, Health Care
 and Education

 Luxembourg enjoys a per capita income of about $9,500
 making it one of the most affluent countries in
 Europe. The government seeks to ensure that these
 benefits are distributed equitably throughout the
 economy. Luxembourg pursues a liberal wage and social
 benefit policy. The 1980 budget continues the
 governing coalition's efforts to improve social condi-
 tions by creating more jobs, increasing family
 assistance and aiding the handicapped.
 3. Respect for Civil and Political Liberties, Includ-
 ing:
 a. Freedom of Speech, Press, Religion and
 Assembly
 These freedoms are guaranteed by law and respected
 in practice.
 b. Freedom of Movement Within the Country,
 Foreign Travel and Emigration
 There is freedom of domestic and foreign travel and
 of emigration.
 c. Freedom to Participate in the Political
 Process
 Legislative power is vested in an assembly elected by
 universal suffrage. National elections were held in
 1979 as scheduled. Labor is free to organize and
 strike, and the labor movement is active and through
 its unions and tripartite (labor, government and busi-
 ness) meetings plays an influential role in the Luxem-
 bourg economy. Under the Rome Treaty, men and women
 are guaranteed equal wages. The Government of Luxem-
 bourg, in a recent review of its laws, found no dis-
 crimination against women and has a draft law pending
 guaranteeing women's rights. Luxembourg's active
 participation in the Council of Europe's Human Right's
 Program further assures these rights.
 4. Goverrment Attitude and Record Regarding Inter-
 national and Non-governmental Investigation of
 Alleged Violations of Human Rights:
 The question of outside investigation of the human
 rights situation in Luxembourg has not arisen.
 
 
 MALTA
 In accordance with its Constitution of 1974, Malta is
 a democratic Republic with a parliamentary form of
 government elected by universal suffrage.  It is a
 member of the British Commonwealth. Chapter IV of the
 Constitution guarantees fundamental rights and freedoms
 of the individual to every person in Malta regardless
 of race, place of origin, political opinions, color,
 creed, or sex.
 A President serves as Chief of State, but direction of
 the Executive is exercised by the cabinet under the
 leadership of the Prime Minister. Two parties contest
 the sixty-five seats in the unicameral House of Repre-
 sentatives on the basis of proportional representation.
 The judicial branch is independent.
 Malta has a record of respect for human rights domesti-
 cally and active promotion of human rights internationally.
 1. Respect for the Integrity of the Person, Including
 Freedom from:
 a. Torture
 Freedom from torture is guaranteed by the Constitution
 and respected in practice.
 b, Cruel, Inhuman or Degrading Treatment or
 Punishment
 Freedom from cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment or
 punishment is guaranteed by the Constitution and
 respected in practice.
 c. Arbitrary Arrest or Imprisonment
 Freedom from arbitrary arrest or- imprisonment is guaranteed
 by the Constitution and respected in practice.
 d. Denial of Fair Public Trial
 A fair trial before an impartial court is a right
 guaranteed by the Constitution and respected in practice.
 Although the Constitution allows for the exclusion of
 the public in circumstances where publicity would
 prejudice the interests of justice, this has not affected
 the rights of citizens to a fair trial.
 e. Invasion of the Home
 Freedom from invasion of the home is guaranteed by. the
 Constitution and respected in practice.

 2. Government Policies Relating to the Fulfillment of
 Such Vital Needs as Food, Shelter, Health Care and
 Education:
 The availability to citizens of the highest grades
 of education and of maintenance and social assist-
 ance are declared by the Constitution as fundamental
 aims of government. The present government provides
 extensive social benefits and is committed to pur-
 suit of these aims through a social welfare policy
 which includes old age pensions, health and medi-
 cal services, education reforms, and low-cost
 housing.
 3. Respect of Civil and Political Liberties, Including:
 a. Freedom of Speech, Press, Religion and Assembly
 These freedoms are guaranteed by the Constitution and
 respected in practice.
 b. Freedom of Movement within the Country, Foreign
 Travel and Emigration
 These freedoms are guaranteed by the Constitution and
 respected in practice.
 c. Freedom to Participate in the Political Process
 There is a high degree of participation in Malta's
 democratic two-party system. There is an active trade
 union movement, and the leadership of the majority Malta
 Labor Party is formally integrated in part with the
 leadership of Malta's largest trade union. The Constitu-
 tion guarantees equal rights and wages for women. Women
 participate freely in social and political life and have
 an increasingly important economic role.
 4. Government Attitude and Record Regarding International
 and Non-governmental Investigation of Alleged Viola-
 tions of Human Rights:
 No requests are known to have been made for outside
 investigation of the human rights situation in Malta.
